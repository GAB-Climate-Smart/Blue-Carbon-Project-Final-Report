### ALERT SUMMARY BOX

> ⚠️ **TWO ALERT-LEVEL CONDITIONS REQUIRE IMMEDIATE MANAGEMENT ATTENTION:**
>
> **ALERT 1 — Anyanui-Tunu Road (Keta):** Water pH recorded at **2.7** — extreme acid sulphate water chemistry. This is more than 4 pH units below the productive range for *Rhizophora racemosa* (6.5–7.6). Dead mangroves were directly observed at this station during the December 2025 survey. Acid sulphate conditions arise when previously waterlogged, iron sulphide-rich soils are exposed to air through drainage or hydrological disruption. If the acid drainage plume is expanding, it represents a risk to surrounding mangrove stands with no known short-term remediation pathway. **Action required within 30 days: field investigation and delineation of acid sulphate soil extent.**
>
> **ALERT 2 — Ada-Songor Interior Lagoon (Patukope–Pute and connected clusters):** ORP values of **832–1,547 mV** recorded in the interior lagoon and creek systems of Ada-Songor. These hyperoxidising conditions — substantially above the 200–400 mV range typical of healthy wetland surface water — indicate that aerobic decomposition pathways are dominant in the water column and likely penetrating into the sediment layer. In a blue carbon context, hyperoxidising conditions accelerate the mineralisation of sediment organic carbon, converting stored soil carbon to CO₂. If these ORP values reflect a permanent or seasonal feature of the interior lagoon (rather than a measurement artefact), the Pute lagoon system cannot currently function as a carbon-accumulating ecosystem. **Action required: quarterly monitoring to establish whether ORP values are seasonally variable or structurally persistent; paired sediment carbon measurement to quantify active SOC loss rate.**

### How This Report Feeds the System

**→ Feeds the MRV Framework (National MRV Report):**
The 80-station quarterly monitoring network described in Chapter 10 of this report is directly integrated as the field data layer of the national MRV system's Ecological and Carbon Integrity Layer (Layer I) and Environmental Pressure Surveillance Layer (Layer II). The two ALERT conditions identified in this baseline — Anyanui-Tunu Road pH 2.7 and Ada-Songor interior ORP 832–1,547 mV — are inputs to the MRV system's escalation trigger framework: these are real-world examples of the parameter thresholds that the MRV escalation protocol is designed to detect and route to institutional response. Every quarterly data submission from Community Change Agents at Keta and Ada will be received by the national MRV platform and compared to the December 2025 baseline established by this report.

**→ Feeds the Drone/EOSDA Monitoring Report:**
The 57 Keta monitoring stations and 23 Ada monitoring stations are spatially co-located with the four drone survey Areas of Interest (AoIs) covering a combined 1,467 ha at Keta and Ada. EOSDA satellite platform alerts — triggered when NDMI (Normalised Difference Moisture Index) or NDVI (Normalised Difference Vegetation Index) values at monitored locations shift by more than 15% — will be cross-referenced with the physico-chemical station data from this report to distinguish between spectral changes driven by water chemistry (e.g., salinity stress) and those driven by physical disturbance or vegetation die-back. The Anyanui-Tunu Road ALERT cluster overlaps directly with one AoI boundary, making EOSDA time-series data from that location an important corroborating source.

**→ Feeds the Carbon Inventory Report (Pending):**
The soil health findings of this report — particularly the humus content data (Galotse 40.6%, Tunu 44.3%, Agortoe 37.5% as soil organic carbon proxies) and the ORP-driven carbon loss risk at Patukope-Pute — establish the ecological context within which the formal soil organic carbon (SOC) stock measurements of the Carbon Inventory Report will be interpreted. Clusters rated Good in this report (especially Galotse-Genui and Saviatula-Salo) are the primary candidates for Permanent Sample Plot (PSP) establishment; clusters rated Alert are priority candidates for carbon-loss quantification. The Carbon Inventory Report (external contributor, structure defined in STANDALONE_REPORT_STRUCTURES.md) will convert the condition signals from this assessment into tC/ha and total tC stock figures.
