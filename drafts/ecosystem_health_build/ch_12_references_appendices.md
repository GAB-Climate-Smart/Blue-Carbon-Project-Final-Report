# Chapter 12: References and Appendices

---

## 12.0 Chapter Overview

This chapter provides the scientific credibility layer and the operational support materials for the Ghana Blue Carbon Assessment — Standalone Ecosystem Health Monitoring Report. It comprises the full reference list for all sources cited across the report, seven appendices covering the station coordinate registers, field data collection instruments, condition scoring criteria, and a placeholder register for figures pending insertion. Appendices A through D serve as permanent records of the monitoring network's spatial infrastructure. Appendices E and F are intended as working documents to be printed, laminated, or carried in the field by Community Change Agents (CCAs) and site managers. Appendix G tracks the 20 original figures from the source assessment that are pending reinsertion in the final report layout.

---

**THE FOUR-TIER CONDITION CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM**

The Ghana Blue Carbon Assessment uses a four-tier classification — **GOOD**, **MODERATE**, **STRESSED**, and **ALERT** — applied independently to water quality and soil quality at each monitoring station, then integrated into a cluster-level overall condition score. The fourth tier (ALERT, distinguished from the simpler three-tier "Good/Moderate/Poor" framework) is used because the Ghana dataset contains parameter values that represent qualitatively different ecological states — active, irreversible carbon loss — that cannot be managed equivalently to sites that are merely showing stress signals.

**Any single parameter meeting an ALERT threshold is sufficient to classify the entire station as ALERT, regardless of other parameter values.**

---

**Table F.1: Water Quality Condition Scoring Criteria — Quantitative Thresholds by Tier**

| Parameter | Units | GOOD | MODERATE | STRESSED | ALERT |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| pH | — | 6.0–8.0 | 5.5–6.0 or 8.0–8.5 | < 5.5 or > 8.5 | < 4.0 (any reading) |
| Temperature | °C | < 30.0 | 30.0–33.0 | 33.0–35.0 | > 35.0 |
| Conductivity | mS/cm | Within benchmark range for cluster | Elevated above benchmark | Significantly above benchmark | Extreme; indicative of saline intrusion or pollution loading |
| Salinity (*Rhizophora*) | ppt | 5–35 | 35–40 (upper tolerance limit) | > 40 (hypersaline; persistent) | Sustained hypersalinity combined with other stressors |
| Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) | ppm | Within cluster benchmark | Elevated above benchmark | Significantly above benchmark | Extreme; indicative of evaporative concentration or pollution |
| Dissolved Oxygen (DO) | mg/L | > 5.0 | 3.0–5.0 | < 3.0 (approaching hypoxia) | < 2.0 (hypoxic; fish kill risk) |
| Oxidation-Reduction Potential (ORP) | mV | −200 to +300 (reducing to mildly oxidising) | +300 to +600 (moderately oxidising) | +600 to +800 (strongly oxidising) | > +800 (extremely oxidising; carbon loss risk) |

## 12.3 Data Provenance and Attribution

All field data in this report was collected by the Ghana Blue Carbon Assessment team during the December 2025 baseline assessment at Keta Lagoon Complex (Keta Municipal and Anloga Districts, Volta Region) and Ada-Songor Lagoon (Ada East District, Greater Accra Region). Data collection was conducted under the authority of the Ghana Wildlife Division, Forestry Commission, with support from the WACA ResIP 2 programme managed by the World Bank Group (P175525).

Community Change Agents (CCAs) participated in all field data collection activities, receiving hands-on training in the use of multi-parameter water and soil probes throughout the assessment period. The CCAs identified in the field data sheets are authorised data collectors for all subsequent quarterly monitoring rounds under the monitoring plan established in Chapter 10 and detailed in the quarterly field data sheet in Appendix E of this chapter.

The condition scoring framework applied in this report (Chapter 8, summarised in Appendix F) was developed specifically for the Ghana Blue Carbon context, drawing on the ecological parameter ranges established in the December 2025 baseline data and the tier definitions of blue carbon ecosystem function described in Howard et al. (2014), Donato et al. (2011), and the IPCC (2014) Wetlands Supplement. Verra VM0033 alert threshold criteria (Verra, 2021) informed the MRV escalation thresholds adopted for soil nutrient parameters.
