# Scope Logic Note (QA)

## Purpose
This memo explains scope evolution with updated support from normalized PDF conversion.
It separates: (a) original mandate, (b) implementation refinement, and (c) current deliverable logic.

## 1) Original project mandate (ToR baseline)
- The ToR sets a **national coastal mandate** for assessing blue carbon ecosystems (mangrove and tidal salt marsh), including mapping extent, degradation drivers, carbon inventory, monitoring protocol, restoration strategy, and final synthesis reporting.
- The ToR phase structure is national in intent (7 phases), with explicit outputs for national cover maps and baseline-linked monitoring protocols.

## 2) How literature review + inception refined implementation focus (now better supported)
- The normalized inception extraction now provides direct evidence that an inception report was formally submitted following an inception meeting with MESTI and MLNR, and that approach/methodology/workplan framing was documented.
- The normalized inception text also shows project-governance structure signals (charter, scope, methods, risk, stakeholder and management plans in contents), which supports the claim that refinement occurred through planned governance steps.
- Because OCR quality is uneven, exact wording remains provisional for citation-critical text, but the prior “no extraction visibility” constraint is now reduced.

## 3) Why national extent work can coexist with narrower detailed baselines
- This remains methodologically coherent: national mapping establishes country-level baseline and prioritization frame; detailed socio-economic/ecosystem/carbon/governance baselines can then focus on landscapes with strongest implementation relevance and field depth.
- The normalized ecosystem draft supports this layered logic by showing detailed field protocol/sample concentration around named operational sites rather than full-country uniform sampling.

## 4) Why Muni-Pomadze appears in some phases but not all later technical phases
- Existing evidence still supports that Muni-Pomadze appears in earlier/multi-site framing while later technical depth concentrates where mangrove and monitoring layers are most developed.
- Normalized ecosystem draft strengthens Keta/Songor execution visibility but does not by itself resolve all Muni pathway details.
- This should still be described as **adaptive prioritization within mandate**, not scope abandonment.

## 5) Why Keta is the pilot for project-specific MRV and continuous monitoring
- Sources continue to indicate Keta has dense layered evidence and implementation readiness.
- Normalized ecosystem extraction adds additional operational depth (sample counts, parameter lists, field design timing) reinforcing why Keta-linked pilot implementation logic is defensible.
- Pilot logic must remain framed as sequencing within a national framework.

## 6) How national MRV framework and project-specific hosted baselines fit together
- National MRV design provides architecture/governance/indicator backbone.
- Project-specific hosted baselines (e.g., Keta-focused operational layers) are implementation modules inside that national architecture where data maturity is highest.
- In short: **national framework = standards/governance backbone; hosted site baselines = operational entry points**.

## 7) Reuse wording (cautious, defensible)
- “The consultancy retained a national baseline mandate while applying evidence-led, site-differentiated technical depth.”
- “Inception-stage governance artifacts now have direct (OCR-limited) visibility through normalized extraction.”
- “Keta’s pilot role reflects data maturity and monitoring readiness, not a change in national scope authority.”
