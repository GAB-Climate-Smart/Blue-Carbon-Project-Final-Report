## B. Reviewer confusion points + one-sentence fixes

### Risk 1: Scope authority ambiguity
- **Possible confusion:** Reviewers may think implementation detail documents are redefining scope.
- **Fix:** Use one standard sentence: “Scope authority remains ToR + inception governance context; later technical outputs provide site-differentiated execution evidence.”

### Risk 2: Inception refinement visibility
- **Possible confusion:** Prior drafts said inception extraction was unavailable.
- **Fix:** Update language to: “Inception extraction is now available via normalized markdown, but OCR quality requires PDF-side verification for citation-critical wording.”

### Risk 3: Terminology drift (Songhor/Songor; Ada vs Songor; Keta Lagoon vs Keta Lagoon Complex)
- **Possible confusion:** Site names may look like different geographies rather than naming variants.
- **Fix:** Add/maintain a one-page “Site Name Normalization Table” in QA/annex artifacts.

### Risk 4: Overstatement of national coverage for detailed baselines
- **Possible confusion:** Narrative may imply all detailed baselines are nationally uniform.
- **Fix:** Use recurring qualifier: “Detailed baselines were site-focused within a national baseline framework.”

### Risk 5: Under-explained adaptive project management
- **Possible confusion:** Coverage narrowing may be interpreted as inconsistency rather than sequencing.
- **Fix:** Add a short “adaptive prioritization rationale” paragraph linking national baseline outputs to phased deepening criteria.

### Risk 6: Muni-Pomadze treatment may look contradictory
- **Possible confusion:** Muni appears in governance/risk narratives but less in later technical detail.
- **Fix:** State that Muni remains relevant for governance/restoration-risk analysis even where technical mangrove-depth outputs are lighter.

### Risk 7: Keta pilot language may be over-read as exclusive focus
- **Possible confusion:** “Keta pilot” could be interpreted as excluding other landscapes.
- **Fix:** Pair every pilot statement with “within a national MRV framework applicable to multiple landscapes as baselines mature.”

### Risk 8: OCR-derived confidence overstatement
- **Possible confusion:** Normalized markdown may be treated as fully clean source text.
- **Fix:** Add one line where relevant: “Normalized PDF extraction improved visibility but remains OCR-limited for exact quotations and some numeric strings.”
