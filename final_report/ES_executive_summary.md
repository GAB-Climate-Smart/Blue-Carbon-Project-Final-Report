# Executive Summary

## The assignment in one paragraph

This Final Report consolidates the seven-phase *Assessment of Blue Carbon Ecosystem Potentials of Ghana (Mangrove and Tidal Salt Marsh)*, executed under Contract GR-MESTI-441726-CS-CQS within the World Bank WACA Resilience Investment Project Phase II (P175525), commissioned by MESTI with technical oversight by MLNR. It integrates four standalone technical reports (Ecosystem Health, National MRV Framework, Drone Survey and Continuous Monitoring, Carbon Inventory and Stock Assessment) and the supporting socio-economic, governance, and CREMA readiness baselines into a single decision-oriented document. It is the Phase 7 deliverable.

## What the report establishes

The report establishes the integrated baseline for Ghana's three priority coastal Ramsar Sites (Keta Lagoon Complex, Songor Lagoon, Muni-Pomadze) and the Ada estuary as a comparison system. The Keta Lagoon Complex contains 20,717.95 hectares of mangrove cover, the largest contiguous blue carbon asset in the assignment scope. The full per-site, per-cluster numbers for spatial extent, ecological condition, socio-economic dependency, and tenure split are reported in Chapters 6, 7, 9, and 10 respectively, and the cluster-level register is in Annex B.

## What the report decides

The integrated synthesis in Chapter 12 ranks the priority blue carbon ecosystems on a five-tier scale against six explicit criteria (carbon mitigation potential, ecological condition, tenure feasibility, carbon-weighted overlay, socio-economic dependency, pressure trajectory). The Tier 1 (highest priority) clusters are at the Keta Lagoon Complex, where retained ecological character, high carbon density, CREMA readiness in the short term, and rising pressure together produce the highest-leverage protection opportunity in the assignment. Tier 2 clusters at Songor Lagoon carry the additional leverage of the dual Ramsar / Biosphere designation. Tier 3 clusters are the degraded restoration priorities. Tier 4 covers the Muni-Pomadze peri-urban management test case. Tier 5 covers the parcels where tenure must be reconciled before priority can be re-assessed.

The cost-benefit analysis finds that protection of Tier 1 and Tier 2 clusters is the most favourable benefit-cost intervention category under the central price scenario, that restoration of Tier 3 clusters is the second-most favourable category and is sensitive to the carbon price assumption, and that hydrological reconnection and expansion are the most uncertain intervention categories.

## What the report recommends

The recommendations in Chapter 14 are grouped under four management decisions: protection, restoration, monitoring sustainment, and the carbon-finance pathway. The headline recommendations are: (1) initiate formal CREMA establishment at the Tier 1 Keta clusters within 12 months; (2) initiate Songor CREMA in parallel; (3) reconcile tenure at the Tier 5 parcels within 24 months; (4) sequence Tier 3 restoration after Tier 1 protection, paired with cookstove demand-side rollout; (5) transition the continuous monitoring system to a sustained Wildlife Division host within 12 months; (6) re-issue Ramsar Article 3.2 reporting on the new ecological character baseline; and (7) pursue an Article 6.2 cooperative arrangement for the protection-based mitigation outcomes at Tier 1 Keta clusters in parallel with the Verra crediting pathway.

The master spatial intervention table in Chapter 13 names the location, the priority tier, the intervention type, the implementation timeframe, and the lead and supporting partners for each recommendation. It is the document that the Phase 7 ToR explicitly names as the principal Final Report deliverable.
