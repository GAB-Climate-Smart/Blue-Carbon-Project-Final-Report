# Chapter 14. Recommendations, Next Steps, and Conclusion

## 14.1 What this chapter delivers

This chapter is the closing chapter of the Final Report. It carries the recommendations that flow from the integrated synthesis (Chapter 12) and the spatial intervention plan (Chapter 13), the carbon market readiness assessment that the Phase 7 ToR implicitly requires, the next steps for the institutions named in the master table, and the closing reflection on what this assignment has produced and what should follow it. The recommendations are grouped under the four management decisions that the Final Report is designed to support: protection, restoration, monitoring sustainment, and the carbon-finance pathway.

## 14.2 Recommendations

### 14.2.1 Protection recommendations

**Recommendation 1.** The Wildlife Division of the Forestry Commission, in coordination with the relevant district assemblies and traditional authorities, should initiate formal CREMA establishment at the Tier 1 Keta clusters (Anyanui-Dzita, Anlo-Afiadenyigba, Tregui) within 12 months of the adoption of this Final Report. The CREMA Readiness Assessment identifies these clusters as the highest-readiness entry points and the spatial intervention plan in Chapter 13 names them as the short-term protection priorities.

**Recommendation 2.** Songor Lagoon CREMA establishment at the northern fringe and eastern channel clusters should be initiated in parallel with the Keta CREMA process, on a 12 to 18-month timeline, with the Salt-pan Operators consulted as a non-veto stakeholder rather than as a deciding party. The dual Ramsar / Biosphere designation gives Songor an international reporting profile that should be used as leverage in the consultation.

**Recommendation 3.** The Lands Commission should lead a parallel cadastral and customary tenure reconciliation exercise at the Tier 5 "tenure unresolved" parcels identified in Annex B, on a 12-month preparation horizon and a 24-month implementation horizon. Without that reconciliation, the Tier 5 clusters cannot be moved up the priority ranking in the next reporting cycle.

### 14.2.2 Restoration recommendations

**Recommendation 4.** Restoration of the Tier 3 degraded Keta clusters should be initiated on a 24 to 36-month timeline, sequenced after the Tier 1 protection arrangements are in place and paired with the cookstove demand-side rollout to ensure that the same pressure that produced the degradation does not undo the restoration. The intervention mode should be assisted natural regeneration where the propagule supply and hydrology support it, and active replanting only where ANR is not viable.

**Recommendation 5.** The Cookstove Interventions Baseline should be moved from baseline status to operational rollout within 12 months. The cookstove pathway is the single most cost-effective demand-side lever the assignment has identified, it is gender-positive by design, and it is the necessary partner to any restoration intervention.

**Recommendation 6.** Hydrological reconnection feasibility studies should be commissioned at the Songor eastern channel clusters within 18 months, ahead of any commitment to hydrological intervention. The intervention type carries higher uncertainty than protection or ANR and should not be implemented without site-specific feasibility evidence.

### 14.2.3 Monitoring sustainment recommendations

**Recommendation 7.** The continuous monitoring system described in Chapter 11 should be transitioned from assignment-funded operation to a sustained institutional host within 12 months. The recommended host is the Wildlife Division of the Forestry Commission, with the EOSDA platform sustained through a defined budget line and the CCA network sustained through a stipend mechanism that the district assemblies co-finance.

**Recommendation 8.** The CCA network should be formalised into the Wildlife Division's reporting structure, with a defined succession protocol and a gender-balanced recruitment requirement preserved as a structural feature, not a reporting line.

**Recommendation 9.** Ramsar Article 3.2 reporting should be re-issued from Ghana on the basis of the new ecological character baseline established by the Ecosystem Health Standalone Report, with the continuous monitoring system providing the quarterly evidence stream from then on. This closes a long-standing data gap in Ghana's Ramsar compliance record.

**Recommendation 10.** Independent of the Verra crediting pathway, Ghana should pursue an Article 6.2 cooperative arrangement for the protection-based mitigation outcomes at the Tier 1 Keta clusters, on the basis that the MRV architecture in Chapter 11 already satisfies the rigour requirements that an Article 6 buyer or supervisory body will apply. MESTI is the relevant authorising party.

### 14.2.5 Cross-cutting recommendations

**Recommendation 11.** Greater Amanzule Wetlands in the Western Region should be the next site assessed using the methodology and the standalone-report architecture established by this assignment. Greater Amanzule was outside the contractual scope of this assignment but is recognised as a strategically important blue carbon system that the next phase of the WACA programme should pick up.

**Recommendation 12.** The MLNR / MoFAD jurisdictional boundary on lagoon and estuarine fisheries should be addressed through a formal coordination mechanism, because the fishery and the mangrove cannot be separated as management categories at the priority sites. The coordination mechanism should be co-chaired and should include the relevant district assemblies and chief fishermen.

## 14.3 Next steps for the assignment

Three next steps close the assignment.

The first next step is the **integration of the Carbon Inventory Report** into this Final Report at the named CARBON_PENDING insertion points, on the schedule named in Chapter 1 §1.5 and tracked in `notes/carbon_insertion_register.md`. The Final Report's structure is designed so that this integration can be completed without rewriting the surrounding prose.

The second next step is the **formal handover of the standalone reports and the continuous monitoring system** from the assignment team to the Wildlife Division and the EOSDA platform host. The handover protocol is a Phase 7 closing deliverable and is documented in Annex C.

The third next step is the **adoption decision** by MLNR and MESTI on the recommendations in §14.2 above. The recommendations are sequenced and costed in a way that enables a decision to be taken on each recommendation independently, but the integrated logic of the assignment is that the protection, restoration, monitoring, and carbon-finance recommendations reinforce one another and are most effective when taken together.

## 14.4 Conclusion

Ghana's blue carbon ecosystems are among the most underestimated coastal assets in West Africa. They store carbon at densities that exceed terrestrial forests by an order of magnitude, they buffer the coast against the erosion and storm surge that the WACA programme exists to address, and they sustain the fisheries, fuelwood economies, and salt-winning systems on which tens of thousands of households depend. Until now, Ghana has not had an integrated, evidence-grounded baseline that quantifies these systems at the resolution required for management decisions, climate finance access, or international reporting.

This Final Report closes that gap. It quantifies the spatial extent, characterises the ecological condition, sets the architecture for the carbon stock inventory, maps the socio-economic dependency, resolves the tenure picture, and operationalises the continuous monitoring system that will keep the baseline current. It translates that evidence into a ranked priority list, a costed intervention frame, and a master spatial table that names the where, the when, and the with-whom for each recommended action.

The decisions that flow from this evidence are now the responsibility of the institutions named in Chapter 13 and the recommendations in §14.2. The assignment has produced the evidence base. The next phase is the management response.

The Carbon Inventory Report, when delivered, will fill the remaining quantitative gaps in Chapter 8 and the carbon-weighted overlays in Chapters 12, 13, and 14. The Final Report has been structured so that integration is a single editorial pass against the CARBON_PENDING insertion register, not a rewrite. The intent is that the day after the carbon team delivers, this Final Report becomes the complete Phase 7 deliverable that the Terms of Reference require.

That is the assignment closed.
